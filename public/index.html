Dangers of Bad Air

We breathe everyday. That means billions of air particles course through our lungs daily. If that is the case, then what happens when that air becomes polluted?

The AQI

The AQI, or Air Quality Index, is an indicator of cleaniness in the air. The higher the index, the worse the air quality. It ranges from 0 to 500.

0-50 Good
51-100 Moderate
101-150 Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups
151-200 Unhealthy
201-300 Very Unhealthy
301-500 Harzardous

Effects on Health

  • Short-term
    • Damaged lungs
    • Difficulty breathing
    • Additional stress on heart and lungs
    • Coughing, wheezing, tightness in chest

  • Long-term
    • Loss of lung capacity and function
    • Respiratory diseases such as asthma, bronchitis, and cancer
    • Shorter life span

Types of Pollution

  • Ozone

    • Ground level organic chemicals mixed with oxide nitrides react with sunlight and becomes smog. This burns the lungs and can cause permanent damage if exposed for a long period of time. Smog is especially prevalent in cities as automobiles expel emissions constantly.

  • Particulates

    • Solid or liquid microparticles that are mixed into the air. The smaller the particle the more dangerous it is for humans to breathe in. A size of 10 micrometers is considered harmful. Particles smaller than 2.5 micrometers are considered extremely harmful as they penetrate deep into the lungs to cause damage to cells and tissue.

Who's at Risk?

  • Children

    • They are at risk because they tend to be outside more than adults. In addition, children in more air-per-pound than adults do so they are harmed comparatively more than adults with the same breath. Lastly their respiratory systems are still developing thus they are more susceptible.

  • The Elderly

    • Their bodies tend to already have decreased functionality, which makes it harder for them to fight off possible infections. The bad air can also worsen preexisting lung or heart conditions they may have.

  • The Sickly

    • They are already susceptible to serious harm due to their conditions, the bad air will only make it worse. It can also cause their conditions to flare up.

  • The Active

    • People who like to exercise a lot outdoors will also suffer the detriments of polluted air because they are breathing hard while they are outside. An increased heartrate from exercise translates to bigger and quicker breaths that cause more pollutants to enter the body.
