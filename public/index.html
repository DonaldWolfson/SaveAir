Dangers of Bad Air

We breathe everyday. That means billions of air particles course through our lungs daily. If that is the case, then what happens when that air becomes polluted?

The AQI

The AQI, or Air Quality Index, is an indicator of cleaniness in the air. The higher the index, the worse the air quality. It ranges from 0 to 500.

0-50 Good
51-100 Moderate
101-150 Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups
151-200 Unhealthy
201-300 Very Unhealthy
301-500 Harzardous

Who's at Risk?

  • Children

    • They are young so their respiratory systems are not as developed as those of adults. They also tend to be more active and outdoor-sy than adults and thus tend to inhale more air.

  • The Elderly

    • The elderly who possess ilnesses such as heart or lung cancer will not survive long in these harsh conditions.

  • The Sickly

    • They are already sick and dying so the bad air will make them even more sick or cause conditions to flare up

  • The Active

    • People who like to exercise a lot outdoors will also suffer the detriments of polluted air because they are breathing hard while they are outside.

Types of Pollution

  • Ozone

    • Ground-level ozone, otherwise known as smog, forms when volatile organic compounds and oxide nitrides react with the sun's rays. This pollutant restricts the airways and causes difficulty breathing.

  • Particulates

    • These are fine particles that are floating in the air. The smaller the particle the more harmful it is as it is able to bypass most of the body's natural barriers.

  • Wildfire Smoke

    • With the recent fires, wildfire smoke is another pollutant that should given attention. Wildfire smoke can travel miles away from their source which is why special attention must be given when dealing with it.
